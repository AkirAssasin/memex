The way we present subjects can immediately change its perception.

Explaining a title with “x meets y” or “game x with twist y” often causes misinterpretation of design choices because the title is compared with other past works with different design intentions. It doesn’t allow space to deviate from those ideas, or allow games to be understood on their own terms.

Often the quality of a game in the genre is judged by its adherence to or ability to emulate aspects of series landmarks. Too often games are about endless pleasure loops — the moment we’re frustrated or confused, we’re taught to see this as a flaw.
