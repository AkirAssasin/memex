Video games can contain a multitude of interlocking complex systems and distilling these into a board game is a unique challenge.

Player interactions commonly found in adversarial games might lead to denial based play - players deliberately preventing others from experiencing key areas of the game.

Be wary of analysis paralysis.

Players should be rewarded to perform micro-management on complex board games. It is found that players are much more likely to remember to do maintenance actions when they are tied to their advancement and potential victory.
